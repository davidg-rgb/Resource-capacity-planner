    3. Auth separation verification (per D-04, PLAT-10):
       - This is already enforced by the proxy.ts update in Plan 01:
         - Platform routes (`/platform/*`, `/api/platform/*`) are in the `isPublicRoute` list, so Clerk middleware does NOT protect them. Platform routes use their own `requirePlatformAdmin()` JWT check.
         - Tenant routes (`/api/*` except `/api/platform/*`) are protected by Clerk middleware, which requires Clerk session tokens. A platform JWT cookie (`platform-token`) is NOT a Clerk session, so Clerk middleware will reject it.
       - The bidirectional separation is architectural — no additional code needed beyond what Plan 01 already did. Document this in the SUMMARY.
       - Note in plan: Auth separation works because:
         a. Clerk middleware protects tenant routes — rejects non-Clerk tokens (platform JWTs are not Clerk tokens).
         b. Platform middleware (`requirePlatformAdmin()`) protects platform routes — rejects non-platform tokens (Clerk cookies are not platform JWTs).
         c. The `isPublicRoute` matcher ensures Clerk middleware ignores platform routes entirely.
